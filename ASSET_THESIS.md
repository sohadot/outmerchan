## The Five Inevitability Mechanisms

### 1. Name = Verb + Rank

*to outmerchant* — to out-trade commercially. *an OutMerchant* — the merchant at the highest tier of commercial sovereignty. The name is not a brand; it is the terminal state the user pursues inside the system. A merchant who scores Captured, Dependent, or Emerging has one natural path forward: **OutMerchant**. The referential closure: the standard cannot be used without the asset's name becoming the highest goal. (DEC-001, protected by the quality gate.)

### 2. Owning the Measurement

Who owns the measure owns the discussion. The Merchant Sovereignty Score — 8 dimensions, 4 tiers, terminal tier carrying the asset's name, governed question bank, JSON model, quality gate protecting the inviolable elements — makes the category measurable *in the asset's own language*. This is what converts a "site" into a "standard."

### 3. Owning the Lexicon

Every strong category needs a dictionary. The canonical lexicon (Merchant Sovereignty, Route-to-Buyer Control, Platform Capture, AI Commerce Readiness, Machine-Readable Trust, Portable Reputation, Settlement Exposure, Governance Independence, Non-Aligned Commerce, Agent-Selectable Merchant) — defined at `/lexicon/`, bound to the Score, machine-readable via structured data — builds something more durable than SEO pages: **the language of the category.** Lexicon growth is governed by the admission law; terms without system function are deferred, not collected (DEC-011).

### 4. The Interface as Argument

The interface does not decorate the asset. It must say, visually: old commerce is a pyramid in which the platform owns what sits above the merchant; OutMerchant inverts the pyramid — every layer serves the merchant and none owns him. Any motion that does not explain this is deleted. Any effect that makes the project read as gaming or crypto-hype is rejected.

**Rule: concept first, performance second, beauty third.**

Corollary: the doctrine surface (`/`) may carry atmosphere; the instrument surface (`/score/`) must be the opposite — clinic, not showroom: fast, restrained, indexable, accessible.

### 5. Legibility to AI

The future is not only Google. It is Google + AI agents + shopping agents + research agents + analyst agents. Commerce is becoming agent-mediated (structural claim; empirical specifics are sourced per `CLAIM_POLICY.md`). OutMerchant must therefore be **human-readable + machine-readable + agent-readable**. JSON-LD, DefinedTermSet, structured score results, sitemap, canonical, clean HTML are not technical details. They are part of sovereignty.

## Monetization Sequence (Phase 7 — gated by DEC-005)

Rule: **revenue must raise or preserve trust; any income that contaminates reference authority is rejected.** No cheap ads, no random affiliate, no crypto promotions, no tokens, no NFT hype, no sponsored junk.

Opens only after: indexation, impressions, instrument usage, reference pages, initial trust. Then, in order of dignity: paid diagnostic reports (measurement always free — the *report* is paid), premium templates and playbooks, curated registry (curated, never a cheap directory), respectful sponsorship of reference sections with strict editorial separation, intelligence brief, audit intake, and later — once usage exists — Score API, embeddable badge, AI-readable trust profiles. Price points are decided at Phase 7 with data, never fixed in advance.
