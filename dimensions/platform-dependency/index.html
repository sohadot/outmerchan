Platform Dependency

What This Dimension Means

Platform Dependency measures how much of your trade is exposed to a single platform's unilateral decisions. One algorithm change, one policy update, one account suspension — the dimension asks how much of your commerce survives a decision you did not make and cannot appeal in time.

Why It Matters to Merchant Sovereignty

Dependency is where capture begins. A merchant whose revenue flows through one marketplace is not a business partner of that marketplace; they are a tenant. The platform sets the rent (fees), the visibility (ranking), and the right to exist (account status). Every other dimension of sovereignty is weakened when this one is weak, because the platform that controls your route to the buyer can override your decisions on all of them.

How the Score Measures It

The Merchant Sovereignty Score assesses this dimension through 3 governed questions, weighted at 12.5% of the total score. The questions are fixed under public score governance — they change only through a recorded decision, never silently:

  • What share of your revenue flows through your single largest platform or marketplace?
  • If your largest channel suspended your account today, how long could you keep selling?
  • Do you operate a sales channel that no third party can switch off?

Each answer is scored from captured behavior (0 points) to sovereign behavior (maximum points). The dimension score is the share of sovereign points earned, from 0 to 100.

Low Control vs. High Control

What low control looks like

More than 80% of revenue through one platform. No sales channel that a third party cannot switch off. An account suspension means the business stops the same day. Channel strategy is whatever the platform's algorithm rewards this quarter.

What high control looks like

No single channel carries more than a quarter of revenue. At least one owned channel — a property no third party can switch off — produces meaningful income. A suspension on any one platform is an inconvenience, not an extinction event.

One Next Action

Map your revenue by channel this week. If one platform carries more than half, open one channel that no third party can switch off — your own storefront on your own domain — and route real traffic to it before you need it.
