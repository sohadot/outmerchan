Governance Independence

What This Dimension Means

Governance Independence measures who writes the rules of your commerce: you, or a terms-of-service document that changes without your consent. It asks how many parties can rewrite your operating conditions unilaterally — and whether you hold a documented exit path from each of them.

Why It Matters to Merchant Sovereignty

This is the deepest dimension, because it governs all the others. A merchant can diversify channels, own customer data, and run multiple rails — and still operate entirely inside rule systems that others rewrite at will. Sovereignty's final test is not whether you can operate today, but whether you decide the terms on which you operate tomorrow. Every layer should serve the merchant; this dimension measures whether any layer owns him.

How the Score Measures It

The Merchant Sovereignty Score assesses this dimension through 2 governed questions, weighted at 12.5% of the total score. The questions are fixed under public score governance — they change only through a recorded decision, never silently:

  • Who can change the rules of your commerce without your consent?
  • Do you have a documented exit path from each critical provider you depend on?

Each answer is scored from captured behavior (0 points) to sovereign behavior (maximum points). The dimension score is the share of sovereign points earned, from 0 to 100.

Low Control vs. High Control

What low control looks like

Several providers can change fees, policies, or access at any time, with no notice and no negotiation. Dependencies have never been mapped. There is no exit plan from anything.

What high control looks like

Terms are set or genuinely negotiated by the merchant. Critical dependencies are mapped. A documented, tested exit path exists for every provider whose failure or hostility could stop the business.

One Next Action

List every provider whose decision could stop your business, and write a one-page exit path for the most critical one this month. An exit you have documented is leverage; an exit you have tested is sovereignty.

Persistent low control on this dimension is characteristic of the OutMerchant tier. High control across all eight dimensions defines the standard's protected highest rank: OutMerchant.
