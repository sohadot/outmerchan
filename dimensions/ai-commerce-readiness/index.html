AI Commerce Readiness

What This Dimension Measures

AI Commerce Readiness measures whether an AI buying agent can find, parse, and trust your commerce — without a platform feeding it your data. The dimension code is AGT. It asks three precise questions: Is your catalog machine-readable? Are your commercial terms structured and parseable? Can an AI agent verify your identity and trust signals from sources it controls, not sources a platform controls?

A merchant who answers no to all three exists exclusively inside a platform's proprietary information architecture. When AI agents route buyers, that merchant is invisible to every agent that doesn't receive a data feed from their platform. They have traded one form of platform dependency for its next-generation equivalent — and done so invisibly, while believing themselves to be independent.

Why AI Agents Cannot Read Most Merchant Storefronts

Most e-commerce storefronts are built to be read by humans. Navigation, layout, imagery, and prose descriptions are designed for cognitive processing, not machine parsing. An AI buying agent encountering a standard storefront faces the same challenge as a screen reader encountering a scanned PDF: the information is technically present, but structurally inaccessible.

The problem compounds at the trust layer. An AI agent that can parse product and pricing data still needs to verify whether a merchant is credible. For merchants whose reviews exist only on Amazon, whose business identity is asserted only through a platform profile, and whose terms are buried in unstructured checkout flows — there is nothing for an agent to verify independently. The agent cannot confirm the merchant is real, trusted, or operating on the terms the buyer expects.

The result is a machine-legibility gap. Merchants without machine-readable infrastructure are not merely less visible in agentic commerce — they may become less discoverable and less evaluable inside it. As AI-mediated purchasing grows as a share of digital commerce, this gap can compound. The merchants who build machine legibility early accumulate discovery advantages; the merchants who do not risk being underrepresented in a routing layer they cannot yet influence.

The Platform AI Trap: Being machine-readable through a platform's proprietary AI feed is not AI commerce readiness — it is AI platform dependency. A merchant whose catalog is readable only through Amazon's AI layer, Google Shopping, or a marketplace's internal recommendation engine has traded human platform dependency for machine platform dependency. The agent that finds them does so because a platform decided to include them. That remains captured infrastructure.

The Four Layers of Machine Legibility

Agent-selectability requires legibility at four distinct layers. A gap at any layer breaks the chain — an agent that can find a catalog but cannot verify the merchant's trust signals cannot route a buyer to them with confidence.

01

Catalog Legibility

Products, variants, and attributes published in structured formats — schema.org Product markup, open feeds, or structured APIs — that any agent can parse without platform intermediation.

02

Terms Legibility

Pricing, shipping, returns, and warranty conditions published in structured or consistently formatted text. An agent must determine what buying from you actually costs and what happens if the buyer needs to return.

03

Identity Legibility

Business identity asserted through independently verifiable sources: structured Organization markup, registered business profiles, and domain-based identity signals — not just presence on a platform.

04

Trust Legibility

Reviews, trading history, and trust signals accessible from sources an agent can query independently. Platform-locked reviews do not satisfy this layer — an agent checking independently cannot read them.

AI Commerce Readiness vs. Dependence on AI Platforms

There is a critical distinction between being ready for AI commerce and being dependent on AI platforms. The goal defined by this dimension is the former: being machine-legible and agent-selectable on the merchant's own infrastructure — not being recommended by an AI that a platform controls.

Marketplace AI recommendation engines are layers platforms built on top of their proprietary data. A merchant who appears in these surfaces is not agent-selectable — they are platform-recommended. The selection decision belongs to the platform's AI, running on the platform's data, optimized for the platform's objectives. This is a structural extension of platform capture into AI-mediated commerce.

The sovereign position is different: a merchant whose catalog, terms, identity, and trust signals are openly structured and independently verifiable can be selected by agents that no platform controls — agents built by buyers, by enterprises sourcing vendors, by browsers, or by autonomous purchasing systems. That is the readiness this dimension measures.

How the Score Measures It

The Merchant Sovereignty Score assesses this dimension through 3 governed questions, weighted at 12.5% of the total score. The questions are fixed under public score governance — they change only through a recorded decision, never silently:

  • Is your catalog machine-readable — structured data or feeds an AI agent could parse outside any platform?
  • Are your commercial terms — pricing, shipping, returns — published in a structured form an AI agent could parse?
  • Could an AI buying agent verify your identity and trust signals today, without asking a platform?

Each answer is scored from captured behavior (0 points) to sovereign behavior (maximum points). The dimension score is the share of sovereign points earned, from 0 to 100.

What Agent-Selectable Means in Practice

Being agent-selectable is not a technical certification — it is a structural condition. The merchant has built infrastructure that makes them evaluable by software. That infrastructure is also, not coincidentally, the same infrastructure that improves organic search visibility, builds buyer trust in independent research, and makes the merchant portable across platforms if they ever need to migrate.

The practical actions are specific: add schema.org markup to every product page, ensuring Product, Offer, and Organization types are complete and accurate. Publish a structured product feed in a format that doesn't require a platform account to access. Write shipping, returns, and pricing policies in structured sections with consistent formatting — not embedded in checkout flows. Build a review presence on independent platforms, not exclusively on marketplaces. Verify business identity through structured directories and domain-linked Organization markup.

Each of these actions produces compounding returns. Machine legibility built for AI agents also improves organic search visibility, builds buyer trust, and creates the portability that characterizes the Outmerchant tier. None of it requires platform permission.

Low Control vs. High Control

What low control looks like

No structured catalog data outside a platform's proprietary system. Terms exist as prose in checkout flows. Reviews exist only on marketplace profiles. An AI agent operating independently finds nothing it can verify — this merchant does not exist for agent-driven demand.

What high control looks like

Catalog fully structured with schema.org markup and an open feed. Pricing, shipping, and returns published in parseable form on the merchant's own domain. Identity and trust signals independently verifiable across multiple sources. Any AI agent can find, evaluate, and route buyers to this merchant without a platform in the loop.

One Next Action

Add schema.org Product and Offer markup to your three highest-revenue product pages this week. This is the minimum viable step for catalog legibility — the first layer an agent checks. It takes hours, requires no platform permission, and starts building the machine-legible infrastructure that compounds over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AI commerce readiness for merchants?
AI commerce readiness is a merchant's ability to be found, evaluated, and selected by AI buying agents without relying on a platform intermediary. A ready merchant has a machine-readable catalog, structured commercial terms, and independently verifiable trust signals — so any AI agent can process their offer without platform mediation.
How do AI agents choose which merchants to recommend?
AI buying agents choose merchants through a four-layer process: discovery (can the agent find the merchant's catalog?), parsing (can it read structured product and pricing data?), trust assessment (can it verify identity, reviews, and commercial terms from independent sources?), and routing (does the merchant meet the buyer's stated criteria?). Merchants who exist only inside a platform's proprietary feed are invisible to agents operating outside that platform.
What is machine-readable commerce?
Machine-readable commerce means a merchant's catalog, pricing, shipping, returns, and identity are published in structured formats — such as schema.org Product and Offer markup, open product feeds, or structured APIs — that software can parse without human interpretation. A machine-readable storefront is one an AI agent can evaluate without a platform feeding it the data.
How do I make my store visible to AI shopping agents?
To make your store visible to AI shopping agents: add schema.org Product, Offer, and Organization markup to your product pages; publish an open product feed outside any single platform; publish your shipping, returns, and pricing policies in structured or consistently formatted text; and ensure your business identity and trust signals are verifiable from sources an agent can query independently — such as independent review platforms and structured business profiles.
What is structured data for merchants?
Structured data for merchants refers to catalog and business information published in machine-parseable formats. This includes schema.org markup (Product, Offer, Organization, Review), open product feeds (CSV, XML, JSON), and published commercial terms that follow a consistent, queryable structure. Structured data lets software — including AI buying agents and search engines — read and evaluate a merchant's offer without human mediation.
What is an agent-selectable merchant?
An agent-selectable merchant is a merchant whose commerce is legible and trustworthy to AI buying agents operating independently of any platform. They have a machine-readable catalog, structured commercial terms, and independently verifiable identity and reviews. An agent can find them, parse their offer, verify their credentials, and route buyers to them — without needing a platform to mediate the relationship.
How does the Merchant Sovereignty Score measure AI commerce readiness?
The Merchant Sovereignty Score measures AI commerce readiness through dimension 07 (AGT), weighted at 12.5% of the total score. Three governed questions assess: whether the catalog is machine-readable, whether commercial terms are structured and parseable, and whether identity and trust signals can be verified by an AI agent without platform mediation. Scores below 30 indicate the merchant is invisible to agent-driven commerce; scores above 70 indicate strong agent-selectability.
