## FAQ And Structured Data

Visible question-answer blocks are used for reference clarity, AI readability, and extractable explanations. No page was framed as making search-result excerpt promises. FAQ JSON-LD was reviewed against visible text on the enriched pages and remains aligned in purpose and wording.

## Claim Safety Findings

Strong AI-agent claims were reviewed and disciplined. Absolute or overly broad phrasing was softened:

- "cannot read a PDF" became structured-signal dependency language.
- "invisible to all agents" became reduced visibility outside proprietary data access.
- "any agent can access" became more likely discovery and evaluation outside a single platform.
- "structurally excluded" became a machine-legibility gap with bounded compounding risk.
- "Any agent can find, parse, evaluate..." became more likely discovery and evaluation.

The thesis remains intact: agentic commerce creates a new route-to-buyer control layer, but the asset now states this as a governed structural risk rather than a prophecy.

## Drift Review

Sprint 3C did not introduce monetization, buyer outreach, sale posture, marketplace claims, affiliate language, consulting offers, newsletter capture, email capture, analytics dependency, external JavaScript dependency, crypto-hype language, generic SEO blog framing, or pages created only for volume.

## Orthography Review

Official spelling is Outmerchant for the asset, protected rank, protocol, public standard, generated pages, metadata, reports, and governance documents. Lowercase outmerchant remains reserved for the verb, lexical headword, domain, and path contexts. The prohibited forms are governed by `validate_orthography.py`.
