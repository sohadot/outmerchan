Platform dependency

Structural reliance on a single platform for revenue, discovery, fulfilment, or operational continuity.

Full definition

Platform dependency is the structural condition in which a merchant’s ability to trade is contingent on the continued participation of a specific platform. Dependency is measured across four axes: revenue (what share of gross revenue requires the platform to function), discovery (whether buyers can find the merchant without the platform), fulfilment (whether orders can be processed and delivered without platform logistics), and operational continuity (whether the business can function if the platform withdraws access). Platform dependency is one of the eight dimensions scored by OutMerchant.

Usage note

Platform dependency is written in lowercase mid-sentence: “the merchant’s platform dependency score” not “the merchant’s Platform Dependency score.” When used as a compound modifier before a noun, no hyphen is required: “platform dependency risk,” “platform dependency exposure.”
