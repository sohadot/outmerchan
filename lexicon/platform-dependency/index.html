Platform Dependency

Definition

Platform Dependency is the degree to which a merchant’s revenue, visibility, buyer relationships, and operational continuity are contingent on the continued goodwill and operational decisions of a single platform.

Context

Dependency is measured by concentration and replaceability. A merchant with 90 % of revenue from one marketplace, no direct buyer relationships, and catalogue locked in proprietary format has high dependency regardless of how well the platform currently treats them. The risk is structural, not behavioural — it exists independent of current platform conduct.

Related terms

  • Platform Capture
  • Merchant Sovereignty
  • Route-to-Buyer Control
