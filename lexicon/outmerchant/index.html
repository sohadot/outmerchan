OutMerchant

The scoring system that measures merchant sovereignty across eight structural dimensions.

Full definition

OutMerchant is a structured scoring system that evaluates a merchant’s structural independence from platform control across eight dimensions: platform dependency, route-to-buyer control, portable reputation, operational independence, data ownership, financial independence, governance independence, and agentic readiness. The system produces a composite sovereignty score and a level classification (Captured, Dependent, Resilient, or Sovereign) that describes the merchant’s overall structural position.

Usage note

OutMerchant refers to the scoring system itself, not to an individual’s status. A merchant “uses OutMerchant” or “has an OutMerchant score”; they are not themselves “an OutMerchant.” The name is always written as a single compound word with a capital O and capital M: OutMerchant, never Outmerchant, outmerchant, or Out Merchant.
