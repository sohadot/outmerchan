Non-Aligned Commerce

Definition

Non-Aligned Commerce is trade conducted outside the commercial logic of dominant platforms — where the merchant’s interests, pricing, and buyer relationships are not subordinated to platform growth objectives.

Context

The term borrows from non-alignment in geopolitics: the refusal to subordinate one’s interests to a dominant bloc’s logic, without necessarily being anti-bloc. A non-aligned merchant may use platforms; they do not allow platform objectives to determine their commercial decisions. Non-aligned commerce is the practice; Merchant Sovereignty is the condition.
