Non-aligned commerce

Trade conducted through infrastructure that is not controlled by any single platform, marketplace, or intermediary with the structural power to unilaterally alter the conditions of exchange.

Full definition

Non-aligned commerce describes trade that flows through infrastructure no single actor controls. A merchant conducting non-aligned commerce processes payments through portable payment infrastructure, reaches buyers through owned or open channels, fulfils orders through logistics not controlled by a marketplace, and holds buyer data in systems the merchant owns. Non-aligned commerce is not commerce without intermediaries — it is commerce without captive intermediaries. The merchant may use many services, but no single service’s withdrawal stops the business from trading.

Usage note

Non-aligned commerce is written with a hyphen: “non-aligned,” not “nonaligned” or “non aligned.” Lowercase mid-sentence.
