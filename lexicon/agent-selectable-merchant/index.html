Agent-Selectable Merchant

Definition

An Agent-Selectable Merchant is a merchant whose products, pricing, availability, identity, and terms of trade are expressed in forms that AI agents can discover, evaluate, and act on without requiring human mediation.

Context

Selectability by agents is the practical test of AI Merchant Sovereignty. A merchant may believe they are operating independently, but if their commercial information is not in forms that agents can parse and act on, they are not selectable in agent-mediated commerce. The agent-selectable merchant has done the structural work: structured product data, machine-readable pricing, verifiable identity, and transactional infrastructure that does not require a human in the loop.
