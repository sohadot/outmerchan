Agent-selectable merchant

A merchant whose offers, policies, and trust signals are sufficiently machine-readable that an AI agent can evaluate and select them without human mediation.

Full definition

An agent-selectable merchant has structured their commercial presence so that an AI agent acting on behalf of a buyer can discover, evaluate, and transact with them without requiring human interpretation at each step. This requires machine-readable product data, structured policy documents (returns, guarantees, terms), verifiable trust signals, and a transaction flow that can be completed by an agent without human handoff. Agent selectability is the practical expression of agentic readiness at the individual merchant level.

Usage note

Agent-selectable is always hyphenated: “an agent-selectable merchant,” “agent-selectable infrastructure.” Lowercase mid-sentence.
