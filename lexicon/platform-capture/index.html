Platform Capture

Definition

Platform Capture is the condition in which a merchant’s pricing, promotional, and governance decisions are effectively made by the platform rather than by the merchant — through contractual obligation, algorithmic enforcement, or structural dependency.

Context

Capture is distinct from dependency. A merchant can be dependent on a platform (high revenue concentration) without being captured (still sets their own prices, terms, and promotional decisions). Capture occurs when the platform’s rules effectively override the merchant’s commercial judgment — when the merchant’s decisions are constrained to the choices the platform permits.
