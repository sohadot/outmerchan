Platform capture

The state in which a platform’s unilateral decisions can materially impair or end a merchant’s ability to trade.

Full definition

Platform capture describes the condition in which a merchant has become so structurally embedded within a platform’s ecosystem that the platform’s unilateral decisions — on fees, policies, algorithmic visibility, or account status — can materially impair or end the merchant’s ability to trade. Capture is not a metaphor for inconvenience; it describes a structural power asymmetry in which the platform holds the conditions of the merchant’s commercial existence.

Usage note

Platform capture is written in lowercase mid-sentence: “the risk of platform capture” not “the risk of Platform Capture.” The term describes a structural condition, not a brand or product name.
