Governance Independence

Definition

Governance Independence is the degree to which a merchant sets and enforces their own commercial rules — pricing policy, returns policy, dispute resolution, and terms of trade — rather than operating under rules imposed unilaterally by a platform.

Context

Governance is the framework within which every transaction occurs. A merchant who cannot set their own returns policy, who must accept platform dispute resolution as final, or who has no direct legal relationship with their buyers has surrendered the governance of their own trade. Independence here is not about avoiding all external rules — legal requirements apply regardless — it is about ensuring that commercial rules reflect the merchant’s judgment, not a platform’s unilateral policy.
