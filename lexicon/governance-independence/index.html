Governance independence

The condition in which a merchant’s ability to trade is not materially dependent on the policy decisions, content moderation choices, or enforcement actions of any single platform.

Full definition

Governance independence describes the structural condition in which a merchant’s commercial operation is not subject to unilateral termination, restriction, or material alteration by a single platform’s governance decisions. A merchant with high governance independence trades across multiple channels, holds their primary buyer relationships outside any single platform’s system, and has operational continuity that survives a platform suspension or policy change. Governance independence is one of the eight dimensions scored by OutMerchant.

Usage note

Governance independence is written in lowercase mid-sentence: “the merchant’s governance independence score” not “the merchant’s Governance Independence score.”
