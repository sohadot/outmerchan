Route-to-buyer control

The merchant’s ability to reach buyers directly, without requiring a specific platform to mediate the connection.

Full definition

Route-to-buyer control measures the degree to which a merchant can initiate and complete a commercial connection with a buyer without platform mediation. A merchant with high route-to-buyer control has direct traffic sources, owned contact lists, and demand generation capability that does not depend on a platform’s algorithm or advertising system. A merchant with low route-to-buyer control is dependent on a platform to surface them to buyers, and cannot reliably reach buyers if the platform changes its ranking, search, or recommendation systems.

Usage note

Route-to-buyer is always hyphenated when used as a compound modifier: “route-to-buyer control,” “route-to-buyer score.” The full phrase “route-to-buyer control” is written in lowercase mid-sentence.
