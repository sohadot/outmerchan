Route-to-Buyer Control

Definition

Route-to-Buyer Control is the degree to which a merchant owns and controls the path between their products and their buyers — including discovery, communication, and transaction — without requiring platform permission at each step.

Context

The route to the buyer is the most fundamental commercial asset. A merchant who reaches buyers only through a platform’s search and recommendation algorithm has no route of their own — they have access to the platform’s route, revocable at any time. Owned routes include direct email relationships, owned web presence with transactional capability, and any channel the merchant controls regardless of platform decisions.
