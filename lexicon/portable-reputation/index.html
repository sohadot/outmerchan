Portable Reputation

Definition

Portable Reputation is trust and credibility evidence that a merchant owns and can carry across platforms — not locked inside a single platform’s proprietary review or rating system.

Context

Platform-held reviews are commercial capital built on rented land. When access to the platform is lost, the reputation evidence is lost with it. Portable reputation exists in forms the merchant controls: independent review platform profiles, published testimonials, case studies on owned properties, or any structured trust evidence that does not depend on platform access to remain visible.
