Machine-readable trust

Trust signals that are structured, verifiable, and accessible to AI agents without requiring human interpretation.

Full definition

Machine-readable trust describes the subset of a merchant’s trust signals that can be parsed, verified, and acted upon by an AI agent without requiring a human to interpret them. Structured data formats (schema.org markup, verified credentials, API-accessible policy documents) are machine-readable; unstructured review text, PDF certificates, and image-based trust badges are not. As AI agents become the primary intermediaries in buyer decision-making, machine-readable trust becomes a prerequisite for inclusion in agent-mediated commerce flows.

Usage note

Machine-readable is always hyphenated: “machine-readable trust,” “machine-readable signals.” Do not write “machine readable trust” (unhyphenated) or “Machine-Readable Trust” (capitalised mid-sentence).
