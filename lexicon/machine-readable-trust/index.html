Machine-Readable Trust

Definition

Machine-Readable Trust is trust evidence expressed in structured, programmatically accessible forms that AI agents, automated platforms, and buying systems can interpret without human mediation.

Context

Trust signals that only humans can read are increasingly insufficient. As AI agents make or influence purchase decisions, they need to assess trustworthiness from structured signals: schema.org markup, programmatic access to review aggregates, machine-verifiable identity claims, and structured commercial history. Merchants whose trust evidence exists only as unstructured text or inside platform databases are invisible to these systems.
