AI merchant sovereignty

The extension of merchant sovereignty principles to AI-mediated commerce: the condition in which a merchant remains structurally independent even as AI agents become the primary intermediaries between buyers and sellers.

Full definition

AI merchant sovereignty describes the condition in which a merchant maintains structural independence not only from platform control but also from dependency on any single AI system, agent platform, or machine-readable infrastructure provider. As AI agents become the primary intermediaries in commerce — researching, evaluating, and executing purchases on behalf of buyers — a new form of dependency risk emerges: the merchant who is legible only to one agent platform, or whose machine-readable trust infrastructure is controlled by a single provider, has replicated the dependency structure of platform commerce at the agentic layer. AI merchant sovereignty requires that the merchant’s machine-readable signals, policy structures, and trust infrastructure be portable across agent platforms, not locked to any single one.

Usage note

AI merchant sovereignty follows the same orthographic rules as merchant sovereignty: lowercase mid-sentence, no capitalisation of individual words except “AI.”
