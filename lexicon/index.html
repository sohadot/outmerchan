The OutMerchant Lexicon

Every strong category needs a dictionary. This is the canonical lexicon of Merchant Sovereignty: the governed vocabulary through which the condition is named, measured by the Sovereignty Score, and protected under the OutMerchant Protocol.

Admission to this lexicon is governed by law, not by convenience: no term enters without a definition, a function inside the system, a relationship to other terms, and a place in the measurement. The lexicon a machine reads and the lexicon a human reads are the same lexicon — every definition below is published verbatim from the governed structured record.

outmerchant / OutMerchantverb / nounThe headword: the verb names the act of out-trading by ownership; the noun names the protected highest rank of the Sovereignty Score. Merchant SovereigntyconceptThe condition in which a merchant controls their own route to the buyer: channels, customer relationships, reputation, settlement, margin, and the rules of their commerce — with no single external point of control able to terminate or capture their trade. Merchant Sovereignty ScorenounA 0–100 measurement of a merchant's real commercial independence, assessed across eight dimensions of control and classifying every merchant into four tiers: Captured, Dependent, Emerging, OutMerchant. Route-to-Buyer ControlconceptOwnership of the full path between merchant and buyer — discovery, relationship, transaction, settlement, and post-sale trust. The route to the buyer is the most valuable asset a merchant does not own. Platform CaptureconceptThe end-state of unmanaged platform dependency: the condition in which a platform effectively owns the merchant's margin, reputation, customer access, and continuity of trade. Also the name of the lowest tier of the Sovereignty Score (Captured). Platform DependencyconceptThe degree to which a merchant's trade is exposed to a single platform's decisions — one algorithm change, one policy update, one account suspension. The first dimension of the Merchant Sovereignty Score. Portable ReputationconceptTrading history, reviews, and trust signals that a merchant can carry, prove, and re-deploy outside any single platform's database — reputation as an owned asset rather than a hostage record. AI Merchant SovereigntyconceptThe condition in which a merchant is legible, trustworthy, and selectable by AI agents — in discovery, comparison, trust, and purchase routing — without surrendering sovereignty to AI-controlled market access. Agentic Commerce ReadinessconceptA merchant's preparedness for AI agents as buyers: machine-readable catalog, structured commercial terms, and independently verifiable identity and trust signals. The seventh dimension of the Merchant Sovereignty Score. Machine-Readable TrustconceptTrust signals — identity, history, terms, reputation — published in structured, verifiable form that an AI agent can parse and check without asking a platform to vouch for the merchant. Non-Aligned CommerceconceptCommerce structured to operate across fragmenting trade zones, payment systems, and regulatory blocs without binding the merchant's continuity to any single bloc, platform, or intermediary. Not anti-platform, not anti-state, not anti-bank: anti-single-point-of-control. Settlement ExposureconceptThe concentration of a merchant's payment and payout flows in rails they do not control — where one blocked gateway, frozen payout, or delisted account can stop the entire operation. The fourth dimension of the Merchant Sovereignty Score. Governance IndependenceconceptThe degree to which the merchant — rather than a platform's unilaterally changeable terms of service — writes the rules of their own commerce, and holds documented exit paths from every critical provider. The eighth dimension of the Merchant Sovereignty Score. Agent-Selectable MerchantconceptA merchant whose identity, catalog, commercial terms, and trust signals are sufficiently structured and independently verifiable that an AI buying agent can discover, evaluate, and select them without a platform intermediary vouching for them.
