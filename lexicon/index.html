Lexicon

Definitions of the terms used in the OutMerchant sovereignty scoring system.

  • Agent-selectable merchant — A merchant whose offers, policies, and trust signals are sufficiently machine-readable that an AI agent can evaluate and select them without human mediation.
  • Agentic commerce readiness — The degree to which a merchant’s infrastructure supports discovery, evaluation, and transaction by AI agents acting autonomously on behalf of buyers.
  • AI merchant sovereignty — The extension of merchant sovereignty principles to AI-mediated commerce: the condition in which a merchant remains structurally independent even as AI agents become the primary intermediaries between buyers and sellers.
  • Governance independence — The condition in which a merchant’s ability to trade is not materially dependent on the policy decisions, content moderation choices, or enforcement actions of any single platform.
  • Machine-readable trust — Trust signals that are structured, verifiable, and accessible to AI agents without requiring human interpretation.
  • Merchant sovereignty — The condition of trading without structural dependence on any single platform’s decisions, infrastructure, or permission.
  • Non-aligned commerce — Trade conducted through infrastructure that is not controlled by any single platform, marketplace, or intermediary with the structural power to unilaterally alter the conditions of exchange.
  • OutMerchant — The scoring system that measures merchant sovereignty across eight structural dimensions.
  • Platform capture — The state in which a platform’s unilateral decisions can materially impair or end a merchant’s ability to trade.
  • Platform dependency — Structural reliance on a single platform for revenue, discovery, fulfilment, or operational continuity.
  • Portable reputation — Earned trust that travels with the merchant across channels, rather than residing only within a single platform’s review system.
  • Route-to-buyer control — The merchant’s ability to reach buyers directly, without requiring a specific platform to mediate the connection.
  • Settlement exposure — The financial risk arising from a platform’s control over payment timing, reserve policies, and payout terms.
