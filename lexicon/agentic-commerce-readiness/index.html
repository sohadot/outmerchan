Agentic commerce readiness

The degree to which a merchant’s infrastructure supports discovery, evaluation, and transaction by AI agents acting autonomously on behalf of buyers.

Full definition

Agentic commerce readiness measures how prepared a merchant is to participate in commerce mediated by AI agents rather than human browsers. An agent-ready merchant exposes machine-readable trust signals, structured policy documents, and transaction-completable APIs or interfaces that allow an AI agent to evaluate the merchant, verify their claims, and complete a purchase without requiring human mediation at each step. Readiness is not binary; it is measured across three axes: machine-readable trust (structured, verifiable signals), policy legibility (terms and guarantees in structured form), and transaction completability (agent-executable purchase flows).

Usage note

Agentic commerce readiness is written in lowercase mid-sentence. Do not capitalise individual words: “the merchant’s agentic commerce readiness” not “the merchant’s Agentic Commerce Readiness.”
