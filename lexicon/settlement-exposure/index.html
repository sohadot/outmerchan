Settlement Exposure

Definition

Settlement Exposure is the financial risk a merchant carries when revenue from completed sales is held, delayed, or withheld by a platform before being transferred to the merchant.

Context

Settlement exposure is a form of financial dependency that is often overlooked in sovereignty assessments. A merchant who has diversified their sales channels but routes all payment through a single platform’s settlement system has significant exposure: the platform can freeze, delay, or reduce settlement as a lever of control, particularly in dispute situations. Reducing settlement exposure means ensuring that revenue reaches the merchant through channels they control or that have contractually defined settlement terms.
