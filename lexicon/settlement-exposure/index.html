Settlement exposure

The financial risk arising from a platform’s control over payment timing, reserve policies, and payout terms.

Full definition

Settlement exposure describes the financial vulnerability that arises when a platform controls the timing, amount, and conditions of a merchant’s cash receipts. A platform with settlement control can delay payouts, impose reserves on a percentage of receivables, adjust fees before settlement, or freeze funds pending investigation — all without requiring the merchant’s consent and often without meaningful appeal. Settlement exposure is particularly acute for inventory-funded merchants who depend on payout timing to finance their next purchase cycle.

Usage note

Settlement exposure is written in lowercase mid-sentence: “the merchant’s settlement exposure” not “the merchant’s Settlement Exposure.”
