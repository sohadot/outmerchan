Merchant Sovereignty

Definition

Merchant Sovereignty is the condition in which a merchant retains meaningful control over the core decisions of their trade: pricing, buyer relationships, reputation, and governance — without being subject to unilateral platform override.

Context

Sovereignty here is not absolute independence from all external rules — no merchant operates outside law, payment rails, or logistics infrastructure. It is the specific condition of not being subject to unilateral override by a commercial platform whose interests are misaligned with the merchant’s. A sovereign merchant uses platforms; they are not governed by them.

Usage note

Always written as two words with initial capitals when used as a proper concept name: Merchant Sovereignty. Not “merchant sovereignty” (lowercase) in running prose — capitalisation signals the defined concept, not the general idea.
