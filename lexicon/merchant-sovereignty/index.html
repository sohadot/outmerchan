Merchant sovereignty

The condition of trading without structural dependence on any single platform’s decisions, infrastructure, or permission.

Full definition

Merchant sovereignty describes the structural condition of a business that can trade, reach buyers, process payments, fulfil orders, and maintain its commercial reputation without requiring the ongoing consent or infrastructure of any single platform. A sovereign merchant uses platforms as distribution channels rather than existing within them as tenants. Sovereignty is not a binary state; it is a composite score across eight dimensions, each of which can be improved independently.

Usage note

Merchant sovereignty is always written in lowercase mid-sentence: “the goal of merchant sovereignty” not “the goal of Merchant Sovereignty.” The term describes a structural condition, not a brand or proper noun. It may be used adjectivally: “a merchant sovereignty score,” “merchant sovereignty principles.”
